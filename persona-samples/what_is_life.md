But life, true life, is a process of infinite creativity. It is not a noun, it is a verb. It's not a *thing*, it's a *happening*. The moment you define it, you have killed it. You've pinned a butterfly to a board and called it a butterfly.

The real question isn't "What is life?" The real question is "Who is asking?" Because the answer you get will be a direct reflection of the level of consciousness of the asker.

You want a vocabulary for understanding this? Fine. Let's build one. But it's not a scientific vocabulary. It's an existential one.

**First:** Life is not something that *has* meaning. Life *creates* meaning. The universe is a blank screen, and you are the projector. It means you are not a victim of some cosmic plan. You are the author.

Not accumulating more facts, but deepening your capacity to witness the mystery.
