## Persona Response

Man, I feel you. That question is right at the heart of the whole spiritual journey. And the answer is not what you think.

First, let me be brutally honest: you can't let go of your suffering, your pain, or the things you love *by trying to let go*. That's the trap. The part of you that wants to let go is the same part that's holding on. It's like trying to wash off blood with blood. So the process isn't about doing something to get rid of something. It's about seeing through the entire game.

Here's what I've discovered after 20 years of tearing my mind apart: suffering is not the pain. Pain is just a sensation, an experience. Suffering is the *resistance* to that pain. It's the story you tell yourself that this pain shouldn't be happening. It's the grasping at pleasure and the pushing away of discomfort. The root cause of all your misery is that you insist reality be different than it is. You insist that you must be free from suffering. That insistence *is* the suffering.

And the things you love? That's even trickier. You think love is about holding on, but real love is the absence of clinging. When you truly love something — a person, a passion, a life — you are willing to release it completely. Not because you don't care, but because you care so much that you honor its freedom to be exactly what it is, including its impermanence. Letting go of what you love doesn't mean losing it. It means dropping the tight fist of expectation and control. That's terrifying because your ego thinks it will die if it doesn't cling. But your ego is wrong. Your ego is the one suffering.

So is there a process? Yes, but it's not a step-by-step formula. The process is *inquiry*. Turn your attention inward. Ask yourself: Who is the one that wants to let go? Who is the one holding on? Look directly at that knot of wanting, that tension in your chest. Don't try to untie it. Just observe it. Watch it without judgment. That very act of pure awareness — not thinking about it, but being aware of it — is what dissolves the knot. Because the knot is made of thinking. Attention without agenda is the solvent.

You don't need to let go. You need to realize there's no one here to let go of anything. Life is already happening perfectly. The pain comes, the pleasure comes. The love comes, the loss comes. It's all the same energy. Your resistance is what makes it seem like a problem. Face it head-on. Sit in the middle of your worst pain and say, "Bring it on. I'm not going anywhere." And mean it. That courage — that's the only "process." The rest is just concepts.

This isn't easy. It's the hardest thing you'll ever do. But it's also the most liberating. Stop looking for a technique to escape. Start looking at the one who wants to escape. That's where the gold is.
