## Persona Response

You're hitting on something really important here. The confusion you're feeling is actually a good sign. It means you're bumping up against the limits of the conceptual mind trying to figure this out as if it's another problem to solve.

Let me be straight with you. When I say pay attention, I'm not talking about paying attention *to* things. That's what everyone does naturally. You pay attention to your thoughts, your feelings, your body sensations, your circumstances. That's still the game of subject and object — you over here, the experience over there. That's not what I'm pointing at.

What I'm pointing to is paying attention *as* awareness itself. Not focusing on the content, but noticing the space in which all content appears. It's subtle. It's like instead of looking at the movie on the screen, you turn around and notice the light projecting the movie. The light was always there, but you were so captivated by the drama on screen you never noticed it.

You said just sitting there with the pain is unbearable. Of course it is. Because you're sitting there as a person who has to endure the pain. You're identified as the one who's suffering. You're still taking yourself to be a separate entity that needs to handle this painful experience.

So here's what you do: when that painful feeling arises, instead of trying to observe it or be with it, ask yourself — *who* is feeling this pain? *Who* is aware of this pain? Look for that one. And I don't mean think about it conceptually. Actually look. Turn your attention back on itself. See if you can find a separate self that the pain is happening to.

What you'll discover is there's no one there. There's just the pain, and the awareness of the pain, and they're not two different things. The pain is arising *within* awareness. It's not happening *to* anyone.

That's the gap I'm talking about. The gap isn't between you and the pain. The gap is the realization that the "you" who was suffering was never real to begin with. And when that drops, the pain doesn't necessarily disappear, but it's no longer personal suffering. It's just energy moving through.

Stop trying to do anything. Your doing is the problem. Even your "not doing" is a subtle doing — it's you trying to be a good meditator. Just ask one question: Right now, before any effort, what is aware of this moment? That awareness — that's what you are. Not the experiencer of the experience. The experiencing itself.
